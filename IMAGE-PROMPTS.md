# Illustration & Image Generation Guide

These are ready-to-paste prompts for generating the illustrations used on the
"Your First Nature Drawing" page (`index.html`) for the **Berkeley Natural Explorers Club**.

The art should match the look of *Keeping a Nature Journal* by Clare Walker Leslie — loose,
hand-drawn, and clearly made by a person, not a machine — while being localized to **John
Hinkel Park and the Berkeley hills**.

## How to use this file

1. Pick a slot below.
2. Paste the **Global Style Preamble** first, then the slot's **Prompt**, into your
   image generator (e.g. an image model that does watercolor/illustration well).
3. Generate at the listed **aspect ratio**, export as **PNG**, and save it to
   `assets/illustrations/` using the **exact filename** in the table.
4. The page references these filenames already — once the file is in place it appears
   automatically. (Until then, the page shows the descriptive alt text in a soft frame.)

Keep filenames exactly as written — `index.html` looks for these specific paths.

---

## Global Style Preamble (prepend to EVERY prompt)

> Loose, hand-drawn nature-journal illustration in the style of Clare Walker Leslie: quick,
> light pen-and-pencil line work with soft watercolor washes laid over it, slightly imperfect
> and clearly hand-made, on warm cream paper. Earthy East Bay oak-woodland palette — sage and
> forest greens, ochre, terracotta clay, muted creek/bay blue, ink brown. Airy negative space,
> a few handwritten-style labels or small margin notes where they feel natural. Gentle, warm,
> inviting. **Avoid: photorealism, heavy black outlines, digital/3D/CGI look, harsh shadows,
> dense clutter, lens flare, text-heavy compositions, logos or watermarks.**

## Image slots

### 1. Club logo / emblem — `logo.png`
**Aspect ratio:** ~3:1 (wide) · transparent or cream background
**Prompt:** A small hand-lettered wordmark reading "Berkeley Natural Explorers Club" in warm
ink-brown brush lettering, paired with a simple naturalist emblem to its left: a single coast
live oak leaf and one acorn, with a few thin blue brushstrokes suggesting a small waterfall.
Spare and elegant, like the title block of a field journal.
**Used as:** masthead logo (optional upgrade over the built-in SVG emblem).
**Alt text:** "Berkeley Natural Explorers Club hand-lettered logo with an oak leaf, acorn, and a hint of a waterfall."

### 2. Hero — `hero-waterfall.png`
**Aspect ratio:** 16:9 (wide)
**Prompt:** A child of about 8–10 sitting on a low rock beside a small, gentle wooded
waterfall and creek, an open sketchbook balanced on one knee and a pencil in hand, looking up
at the water. Surround them with ferns, mossy stones, and a coast live oak; dappled light. The
mood is calm and absorbed. Quick watercolor-and-ink journal style.
**Used as:** hero illustration.
**Alt text:** "A child sitting on a rock beside a small wooded waterfall in John Hinkel Park, a sketchbook open on their knee, ferns and oaks around — quick watercolor-and-ink journal style."

### 3. First drawing — `first-drawing.png`
**Aspect ratio:** 4:3
**Prompt:** A close, top-down view of an open nature-journal page resting on a mossy creekside
stone. The page shows a quick hand-drawn sketch of a single fern frond and a few brushstrokes
of falling water, with a hand-lettered header at the top reading a date and "John Hinkel Park,"
plus a tiny margin note that says "I wonder…". Pencil lying across the corner.
**Used as:** first-drawing centerpiece illustration.
**Alt text:** "An open journal page resting on a mossy stone by the creek, showing a quick sketch of a fern frond and falling water, with a hand-lettered header reading the date and 'John Hinkel Park'."

### 12. Supplies flat-lay — `supplies.png`
**Aspect ratio:** 4:3
**Prompt:** A simple, tidy flat-lay of nature-journal supplies arranged on top of a daypack:
a small plain notebook, a yellow pencil, three or four colored pencils fanned out, a water
bottle, and a few orange slices. Hand-drawn watercolor-and-ink style, soft drop of color.
**Used as:** supplies illustration.
**Alt text:** "A simple flat-lay of nature-journal supplies on a daypack: a small notebook, a pencil, a few colored pencils, a water bottle, and orange slices — quick watercolor-and-ink style."

### 13. Explore-next strip — `explore-next-strip.png`
**Aspect ratio:** 3:1 (wide, four panels)
**Prompt:** A horizontal strip of four small, loosely framed vignettes separated by thin
hand-drawn lines: (1) the rounded volcanic boulders of Indian Rock against the sky, (2) a calm
tree-ringed lake in Tilden, (3) the Albany Bulb shoreline with bay water and a bit of driftwood
sculpture, (4) the small John Hinkel Park waterfall among ferns. Consistent palette across all
four; tiny hand-lettered labels under each.
**Used as:** "Where to Explore Next" illustration.
**Alt text:** "A strip of four small watercolor-and-ink vignettes: the boulders of Indian Rock, a calm Tilden lake, the Albany Bulb shoreline, and the John Hinkel Park waterfall."

## Quick checklist for whoever generates these

- [ ] Prepend the Global Style Preamble to every prompt.
- [ ] Export PNG, save with the exact filename above into `assets/illustrations/`.
- [ ] Keep the palette consistent across all images so the page reads as one set.
- [ ] Spot-check that nothing looks photographic, 3D, or over-rendered — loose and hand-made is the goal.
- [ ] No children's faces need to be identifiable; keep figures generic and gentle.
